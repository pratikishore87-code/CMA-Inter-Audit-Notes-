## Question 2 — Removal of a Company Auditor
> **PYQ:** MQP 2023-I (Old) | Study Material

### Question
State the provisions of Companies Act, 2013 regarding the **removal of a company auditor**.

---

### Answer — Section 140: Removal of Auditor

A company auditor can be removed from office in the following **two ways**:

---

### Way 1 — Removal Before Expiry of Term (Section 139)

The auditor may be removed **before the expiry of his term** only by:
- A **Special Resolution** of the company, AND
- After obtaining **prior approval of the Central Government**
  (as per Rule 7 of Chapter X of the Act)

#### Procedure:

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | File application to Central Government in **e-Form ADT-2** (with prescribed fees) | — |
| (b) | Application must be made to Central Government | Within **30 days** of Board resolution |
| (c) | Company must hold General Meeting to pass Special Resolution | Within **60 days** of receiving Central Government approval |

> ⚠️ **Proviso:** Before any action is taken, the auditor concerned must be given a
> **reasonable opportunity of being heard.**

---

### Way 2 — Removal by Tribunal (Section 140(5))

The **Tribunal** (NCLT) can order removal of an auditor in the following circumstances:

**(a) Grounds for Removal:**
The Tribunal, either **suo motu** or on application by the Central Government or
any concerned person, may direct the company to change its auditors if satisfied that
the auditor has — directly or indirectly:
- Acted in a **fraudulent manner**, OR
- **Abetted or colluded** in any fraud by/in relation to the company, its directors or officers

**(b) Time Limit for Order:**
If application is made by the Central Government, the Tribunal shall pass an order
within **15 days** of receipt of such application.

**(c) Appointment of New Auditor:**
The Central Government may **appoint another auditor** in place of the removed auditor.

**(d) Consequences for the Removed Auditor:**
An auditor (individual or firm) against whom a **final order** has been passed by the
Tribunal shall:
- Be **ineligible** to be appointed as auditor of **any company** for **5 years**
  from the date of such order
- Also be **liable for action under Section 447** (Fraud)

---

### Comparison Summary

| Basis | Removal Before Term (S.139) | Removal by Tribunal (S.140(5)) |
|---|---|---|
| Who initiates? | Company (Board + Members) | Tribunal / Central Government |
| Approval needed | Central Government | Tribunal order |
| Resolution required | Special Resolution | Not required |
| Timeline | 30 days (application) + 60 days (GM) | 15 days (if CG applies) |
| Grounds | Any reason with due process | Fraud / Collusion |
| Consequence | Removal only | Removal + 5-yr ban + S.447 liability |

---

### Key Sections to Cite in Exam 📌
- **Section 139** — Appointment of Auditor
- **Section 140** — Removal, Resignation of Auditor
- **Section 140(5)** — Tribunal's power to remove
- **Section 447** — Punishment for fraud
- **e-Form ADT-2** — Application for removal

---

### Exam Tip ⭐
Always mention **both ways** of removal in your answer.
The Tribunal route under **S.140(5)** with the **5-year ban** and **S.447 liability**
is a high-scoring point that most students miss.
