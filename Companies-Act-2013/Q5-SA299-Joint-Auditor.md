## Question 5 — Responsibility of Joint Auditor
> **PYQ:** June 2018 | Study Material

### Question
Write short notes on the **responsibility of a joint auditor**.

---

### Answer — SA 299: Responsibility of Joint Auditors

---

### What is a Joint Audit?
A **joint audit** is when **two or more auditors** are appointed together
to audit the same entity. **SA 299** governs how they coordinate,
divide work and share responsibility.

---

### A. Division of Work

**(a) Basis of Division:**
Where joint auditors are appointed, they should by **mutual discussion**
divide the audit work among themselves. Division is usually in terms of:
- **Identifiable units**, or
- **Specified areas** of the business

**(b) Alternative Basis:**
In cases where division by units is not possible due to the nature of
the business, division may be made with reference to:
- Items of **assets or liabilities**, or
- Items of **income or expenditure**, or
- **Periods of time**

**(c) Areas Covered by ALL Joint Auditors:**
Certain areas — owing to their **importance** or **nature of work** —
shall **not be divided** and shall be covered by **all joint auditors** together.

**(d) Documentation:**
The division of work shall be:
- **Adequately documented**, and
- Preferably **communicated to the entity**

---

### B. Co-ordination

Where a joint auditor comes across matters that are:
- Relevant to **another joint auditor's area of responsibility**, or
- Deserve attention, disclosure, discussion or application of judgement
  by other joint auditors

He must **communicate the same in writing** to all other joint auditors —
by way of a **report or note** — prior to finalisation of the audit.

---

### C. Responsibility of Joint Auditors

#### Individual Responsibility
Each joint auditor is responsible **only for the work allocated to him**,
whether or not he has prepared a separate report on that work.

#### Joint and Several Responsibility
All joint auditors are **jointly and severally responsible** for:

| # | Situation |
|---|---|
| (a) | Audit work **not divided** among joint auditors — carried out by all |
| (b) | **Decisions taken by all** joint auditors on nature, timing or extent of audit procedures |
| (c) | Matters **brought to notice** of all joint auditors and agreed upon by them |
| (d) | Examining that **financial statements comply** with disclosure requirements of relevant statute |
| (e) | Ensuring that the **audit report complies** with requirements of relevant statute |

> ⚠️ **Important:** If matters are brought to attention of the entity or
> other joint auditors **after the audit report has been submitted**,
> the other joint auditors will **NOT be responsible** for those matters.

---

### D. Dependence on Other Auditors

- Each joint auditor is **entitled to assume** that the other joint auditors
  have carried out their part of the audit work in accordance with
  **generally accepted audit procedures**
- It is **not necessary** for a joint auditor to:
  - Review the work performed by other joint auditors, or
  - Perform any tests to verify whether work was actually performed
- Each joint auditor is entitled to **rely upon** the other joint auditors
  for bringing to his notice:
  - Any **departure** from generally accepted accounting principles, or
  - Any **material error** noticed during the audit

---

### E. Reporting Responsibilities

- Normally, joint auditors arrive at an **agreed common report**
- Where joint auditors are in **disagreement**, each one should
  express his own opinion through a **separate report**
- A joint auditor is **not bound** by the views of the **majority**
  of joint auditors
- In case of disagreement, he must express his opinion in a
  **separate independent report**

---

### Summary Table

| Aspect | Key Rule |
|---|---|
| Work Division | By mutual discussion — units, areas, items or time periods |
| Undivided areas | Covered jointly by all auditors |
| Documentation | Mandatory — communicated to entity preferably |
| Co-ordination | In writing, before audit finalisation |
| Individual responsibility | Only for work allocated to him |
| Joint & several responsibility | Undivided work, disclosures, audit report compliance |
| Reliance on co-auditors | Allowed — no re-verification needed |
| Post-report matters | Other auditors NOT responsible |
| Disagreement | Each auditor issues separate independent report |

---

### Key Standard to Cite in Exam 📌
- **SA 299** — Responsibility of Joint Auditors

---

### Exam Tip ⭐
Structure your answer into the **5 headings A to E** exactly as above.
The most scoring points are:
- The **joint and several responsibility** table (all 5 situations)
- The **post-report** exception (other auditors not responsible)
- The **disagreement** rule (separate report, not bound by majority)

Always cite **SA 299** — not a section of the Companies Act,
as this is a Standard on Auditing, not a statutory provision.
