## Question 6 — Audit Committee
> **PYQ:** MQP December 2023-I | Study Material

### Question
Explain the provisions of Companies Act, 2013 regarding **Audit Committee**.

---

### Answer — Section 177 of Companies Act, 2013

---

### A. Formation of Audit Committee
As per **Section 177(1)** read with **Companies (Meetings of Board and its Powers)
Rules, 2014**, the following companies are **mandatorily required** to constitute
an Audit Committee of the Board:

| # | Type of Company | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| (i) | All **listed companies** | — (mandatory regardless of size) |
| (ii) | All **public companies** | Paid-up capital of **₹10 crore or more** |
| (iii) | All **public companies** | Turnover of **₹100 crore or more** |
| (iv) | All **public companies** | Outstanding loans/borrowings/debentures/deposits exceeding **₹50 crore or more** |

---

### B. Composition of Audit Committee
As per **Section 177(2)**:

- Minimum **3 directors**
- **Independent directors** must form a **majority**
- **Majority of members** including the **Chairperson** shall be persons with
  ability to **read and understand financial statements**

---

### C. Broad Functions of Audit Committee
As per **Section 177(4)**, the Board shall specify in writing the
**terms of reference** for the Audit Committee, which shall include:

| # | Function |
|---|---|
| (a) | Recommendation for **appointment, remuneration and terms of appointment** of auditors |
| (b) | Review and monitor **auditor's independence, performance** and effectiveness of audit process |
| (c) | Examination of **financial statements** and the **auditors' report** thereon |
| (d) | Approval or modification of **related party transactions** |
| (e) | Scrutiny of **inter-corporate loans and investments** |
| (f) | **Valuation** of undertakings or assets of the company, wherever necessary |
| (g) | Evaluation of **internal financial controls** and **risk management systems** |
| (h) | Monitoring **end use of funds** raised through public offers |

---

### D. Powers of Audit Committee

#### (a) Auditor's Comments — Section 177(5)
The Audit Committee may:
- Call for **comments of auditors** about internal control systems,
  scope of audit and observations of auditors
- **Review financial statements** before submission to the Board
- **Discuss issues** with internal and statutory auditors and management

#### (b) Board's Report Disclosure — Section 177(8)
- The **Board's Report** under Section 134(3) shall disclose the
  **composition of the Audit Committee**
- If the Board has **not accepted any recommendation** of the Audit Committee,
  the same shall be **disclosed in the Board's Report along with reasons**

#### (c) Whistle Blowing Policy — Section 177(9)
Every **listed company** and company **accepting public deposits or borrowing
in excess of ₹50 crore** from banks and financial institutions shall establish a
**vigil mechanism** for directors and employees to report genuine concerns.

#### (d) Safeguards Against Victimisation — Section 177(10)
- The vigil mechanism shall provide **adequate safeguards** against
  victimisation of persons who use it
- Shall make provision for **direct access to the Chairperson** of the
  Audit Committee in appropriate or exceptional cases
- The company shall **disclose establishment** of such mechanism on its
  **website** and in the **Board's Report**

#### (e) Investigation — Section 177(6)
The Audit Committee shall have authority to:
- **Investigate** into any matter specified in Section 177(4)
  or referred to it by the Board
- **Obtain professional advice** from external sources
- Have **full access** to information contained in records of the company

### Exam Tip ⭐
This question has **very high weightage** — it tests both formation
and powers in detail. Structure your answer into the **A-B-C-D**
format (Formation → Composition → Functions → Powers) exactly
as above. Always quote the **sub-section numbers** — examiners
reward precise statutory references.
