## Question 7 — Appointment of Cost Auditor
> **PYQ:** December 2017 | December 2023 (New) | MQP June 2023-II | Study Material

### Question
What is the procedure of appointing a **Cost Auditor** in a company?

---

### Answer — Section 148(3) of Companies Act, 2013 read with
### Rule 6 of Companies (Cost Records and Audit) Rules, 2014

---

### Who Appoints the Cost Auditor?
As per **Section 148(3)**:
- Cost audit shall be conducted by a **Cost Accountant**
- The Cost Accountant shall be appointed by the **Board of Directors**
- The **statutory auditor** appointed under Section 139 shall **NOT** be
  appointed as Cost Auditor of the same company
- The Cost Auditor so appointed shall comply with the
  **Cost Auditing Standards**

---

### Procedure for Appointment — Rule 6

#### Step 1 — Appointment by Board
Companies specified under **Rule 3** and meeting thresholds under **Rule 4**
shall appoint a Cost Auditor within:
> **180 days** from the commencement of every financial year

---

#### Step 2 — Informing the Cost Auditor
Every company shall **inform the Cost Auditor** of his appointment as such.

---

#### Step 3 — Filing with Central Government
The company shall file a **notice of appointment** with the Central Government:

| Detail | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Form | **Form CRA-2** (filed electronically) |
| Fees | As per Companies (Registration Offices and Fees) Rules, 2014 |
| Timeline | Within **30 days** of the Board meeting in which appointment is made **OR** within **180 days** of commencement of financial year — **whichever is earlier** |

---

#### Step 4 — Tenure of Cost Auditor
Every Cost Auditor appointed shall continue in such capacity till:
- **180 days from the closure** of the financial year, OR
- Till he **submits the Cost Audit Report** for the financial year
  for which he was appointed

— **whichever is earlier**

---

### Key Distinctions to Remember 🧠

| Aspect | Statutory Auditor | Cost Auditor |
|---|---|---|
| Governed by | Section 139 | Section 148(3) |
| Appointed by | Members at AGM | Board of Directors |
| Qualification | Chartered Accountant | Cost Accountant |
| Can same person do both? | ❌ NO — explicitly prohibited |
| Form for appointment | — | **Form CRA-2** |
| Filing with | — | Central Government |

### Important Timelines at a Glance ⏱️

| Event | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Appoint Cost Auditor | Within **180 days** of FY commencement |
| File Form CRA-2 | Within **30 days** of Board meeting OR **180 days** of FY start — earlier |
| Cost Auditor's tenure ends | **180 days** from FY closure OR on submission of report — earlier |

---

### Exam Tip ⭐
The **three timelines** — 180 days (appointment), 30 days (CRA-2 filing)
and 180 days (tenure) — are the most frequently tested points in MCQs
and short answers. Also always highlight that the **statutory auditor
under S.139 CANNOT be the cost auditor** of the same company —
this is a common trick question.
